================================================================================
CAUSAL ID FIX 1: INDUSTRY FIXED EFFECTS - ISOLATING REGULATORY EFFECT
================================================================================

Dependent Variable: Volatility Change (%)

Tests whether FCC effect persists after controlling for industry-specific confounds.
If FCC effect reflects industry characteristics rather than regulation, coefficient
should shrink substantially with industry FE. If regulation-driven, effect should persist.

RESULTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Model                                        FCC Coef         SE    P-Value        N       R2
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Baseline (no FE)                            1.7433     0.8953     0.0515      891   0.3888
2. Industry FE (2-digit SIC)                   5.0181     2.2488     0.0257      833   0.4410
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION:
[+] FCC effect STRENGTHENS with industry controls (5.0181 vs 1.7433)
    This suggests FCC effect is NOT driven by industry selection
    Effect is more likely regulatory in nature

Control Variables: firm_size_log, leverage, roa, return_volatility_pre
Standard errors (HC3 heteroskedasticity-consistent) in parentheses.
Significance levels: * p<0.10, ** p<0.05, *** p<0.01
