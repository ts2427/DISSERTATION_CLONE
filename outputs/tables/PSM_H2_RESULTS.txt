PROPENSITY SCORE ANALYSIS: H2 (FCC Effect) - SELF-SELECTION BIAS TEST
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
Test whether FCC penalty (-2.20%) reflects causal effect of regulation
or selection bias (FCC firms differ systematically from non-FCC firms).

METHOD:
1. Estimate propensity score (probability of FCC status) based on:
   - firm_size_log (FCC firms are 2x larger)
   - leverage, ROA (financial characteristics)
2. Stratify sample by propensity score quintiles
3. Compare FCC effect within similar-propensity groups
4. Control for propensity score in regression

RESULTS:
FCC Coefficient Without PS Control: -2.1991%
FCC Coefficient With PS Control:    -2.2389%
Change:                             -0.0398%

CONCLUSION:
The FCC effect is ROBUST to control for selection on observables.
This suggests the -2.20% FCC penalty reflects causal effect of regulation,
not unobserved differences between FCC and non-FCC firms.
