FIX 1: INDUSTRY FIXED EFFECTS - ISOLATING REGULATORY EFFECT
================================================================================

Tests whether FCC effect persists after controlling for industry-specific confounds.
If FCC effect reflects industry characteristics rather than regulation, coefficient
should shrink substantially with industry FE. If regulation-driven, effect should persist.

RESULTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Model                                        FCC Coef         SE    P-Value        N       R2
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Baseline (no FE)                           -2.0788     0.9957     0.0368      653   0.0198
2. Industry FE (2-digit SIC)                  -4.2142     1.6678     0.0115      601   0.0650
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION:
  FCC coefficient change: -2.0788 -> -4.2142 (102.7%)
  Statistical significance: Remains significant in both specifications
  Industry controls: 12 industries with n>=10 observations

  CONCLUSION: FCC effect ROBUST to industry controls.
  The FCC penalty persists even after controlling for industry-specific factors,
  supporting interpretation that the effect reflects regulatory burden,
  not inherent industry characteristics.
